Philosophy

How I think.

I'm drawn to situations where there's no obvious solution. Breaking a complex problem into smaller, understandable pieces is what engineering is really about — and it's the part I enjoy most.

Whether it's debugging a failing API integration, wiring a circuit prototype, or explaining a concept to a 6-year-old, my approach stays the same: understand first, then act.

First principles thinking
Build, test, refine
Learn by doing
Question assumptions
Continuous improvement
Communicate clearly
01
Understand the Problem
Resist the urge to jump to solutions. Define what's actually broken or missing.
02
Decompose
Break complexity into smaller, independently solvable components.
03
Design a Solution
Choose the simplest approach that solves the problem correctly.
04
Build a Prototype
Ship a working version quickly. Imperfect and running beats perfect and theoretical.
05
Test & Refine
Let reality provide the feedback. Iterate until it's right.
